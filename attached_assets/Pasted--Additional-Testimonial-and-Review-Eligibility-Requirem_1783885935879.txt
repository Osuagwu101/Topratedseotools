## Additional Testimonial and Review Eligibility Requirements

The testimonial and review systems must also work when the administrator assigns a tool, product, subscription, or service directly to a registered customer from the Admin Dashboard.

### Admin-Assigned Tool Access

When assigning a tool from the backend, the administrator should be able to:

* Search for and select a registered customer
* Select the tool, product, subscription, or service being assigned
* Set the access start date
* Add an optional expiry or renewal date
* Record an internal reason or note for the assignment
* Choose whether the user should be invited to submit a review
* Choose whether the user should be invited to provide a testimonial
* Revoke or modify the assignment where necessary
* View the history of tools assigned to that customer

The assignment must be stored in the database and linked to:

* The customer’s account
* The assigned tool or product
* The administrator who made the assignment
* The date and time of assignment
* The assignment status
* Any applicable expiry date
* The source of access, such as purchase, renewal, promotion, manual assignment, complimentary access, or administrative correction

A customer with an active administrator-assigned tool should be able to access the relevant review or testimonial form from their account.

## Testimonial Eligibility for Admin-Assigned Users

A customer may submit a testimonial when:

* The customer completed a genuine purchase; or
* The administrator assigned the relevant tool or service to the customer from the backend.

The administrator should be able to send or activate a testimonial request for an eligible customer.

The testimonial record should privately show whether the customer received access through:

* A completed purchase
* A renewal
* An administrator assignment
* Complimentary access
* A promotional allocation

This source information should remain private unless the administrator deliberately chooses to disclose it.

Do not automatically publish testimonials submitted by customers with administrator-assigned access. Every testimonial must still pass moderation and receive manual approval before appearing publicly.

The administrator must also be able to create a testimonial manually after receiving the customer’s permission.

## Reviews for Admin-Assigned Tools

A registered customer should be allowed to review a tool when either:

* The tool appears in a successfully paid and completed order belonging to that customer; or
* The tool has been directly assigned to the customer through the Admin Dashboard and the assignment is active and valid.

The system must prevent customers from reviewing tools they have neither purchased nor received through a valid administrator assignment.

For administrator-assigned tools:

* Link the review to the specific assignment record.
* Confirm that the assignment belongs to the customer submitting the review.
* Confirm that the assigned tool matches the tool being reviewed.
* Prevent multiple reviews for the same assignment unless the administrator permits an updated review after a new assignment or renewal.
* Stop review eligibility if the assignment is revoked before the customer receives or uses the service.
* Keep an audit trail of assignment-based review eligibility.

## Review Badge Accuracy

Do not label an administrator-assigned review as “Verified Purchase” unless the customer also has a qualifying completed purchase for that product.

Use separate, accurate statuses:

* **Verified Purchase:** The customer purchased the reviewed product through a successfully paid and completed order.
* **Verified Access:** The administrator genuinely assigned the tool or service to the customer from the backend.
* **No Public Badge:** The administrator may choose not to display an access badge publicly.

The administrator must not be able to manually apply either badge without a qualifying purchase or valid assignment record.

Publicly displaying a “Verified Access” badge should be controlled through an Admin Dashboard setting.

## Review Prompt for Admin-Assigned Tools

When the administrator assigns a tool to a customer and enables the review request:

* Ask the customer to review the tool when they next log in or return while authenticated.
* Show the request no more than once during the same login session.
* Apply the existing maximum of three separate login-session reminders.
* Allow the customer to submit the review, close the request, or select “Review later.”
* Stop displaying the request after the third ignored or dismissed prompt.
* Stop displaying it immediately after the customer submits a review.
* Reset the prompt only if the tool is reassigned, renewed, or a new eligible access period is created.
* Do not count page refreshes as separate login sessions.

The administrator should also be able to disable a review request for an individual assignment.

## Testimonial Display Controls

Add a master Testimonial Display setting to the Admin Dashboard.

At any time, the administrator must be able to:

* Turn the entire public testimonial section on or off
* Hide the section without deleting existing testimonials
* Restore the section later with its existing content and ordering
* Choose which pages display the testimonial section
* Publish or unpublish individual testimonials
* Preview the testimonial section before enabling it
* Set a maximum number of testimonials displayed on each page
* Reorder published testimonials

When the master testimonial setting is turned off:

* No testimonial section should appear publicly.
* No testimonial content should be returned through publicly accessible frontend endpoints.
* Existing testimonials, permissions, moderation statuses, and ordering must remain safely stored in the database.
* Administrators must still be able to manage testimonials from the backend.
* The setting must take effect without requiring testimonials to be deleted.
* The hidden section must not leave empty spaces, broken layouts, headings, or loading placeholders on the page.

The testimonial display status must be controlled from:

**Trust, Reviews & Support → Testimonials**

Include a clear toggle labelled:

**Display Testimonial Section on Website**

The setting should default to off until at least one genuine, approved, and published testimonial is available.

## Additional Admin Dashboard Requirements

Within the **Trust, Reviews & Support** area, add controls for:

### Tool Assignments

* Assign a tool to a registered customer
* Edit or revoke an assignment
* Search assignments by customer, tool, status, source, and date
* View testimonial and review eligibility
* Enable or disable review invitations
* Enable or disable testimonial invitations
* View reviews linked to assignments
* View testimonials linked to assignments
* View assignment audit history

### Testimonials

* Master public-display toggle
* Page-level display controls
* Individual publish and unpublish controls
* Assignment-linked testimonial management
* Preview before publication
* Permission-to-publish confirmation

### Purchase and Access Reviews

* Reviews linked to completed purchases
* Reviews linked to administrator assignments
* Correct automatic badge assignment
* Filtering by Verified Purchase, Verified Access, or no badge
* Private access to the related purchase or assignment record
* Prevention of false badge application
